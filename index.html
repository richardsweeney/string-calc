    Calculations help

    Home

    In order to calculate the tension of a string you need to know first the diameter of the string, or vice versa. Here's a couple of examples to help you get your head around this.

    Calculating the tension

    Example 1: Calculate the tension of the top string on a lute in kg.

    The highest pitched string on a lute usually has a diameter of around .44 mm. Click on the first string and type 44 in the text field that appears (leave out the decimal point and unit of measurement). Click Calculate Tension to perform the calculation.

    Example 2: Calculate the tension of the top string on a guitar in lbs.

    Let's say the highest string on my guitar is 0.028 inches in diameter. Click on the first string and just enter 0.028 in the text field. Click Calculate Tension to get the tension.

    Calculating the diameter

    Example 1: Calculate the diameter of the e string on a baroque violin in mm.

    Let's say I want to find out how thick a string should use at a a tension of 6 kg. Click on the first string and enter the number 6 in the text field (you can leave out the kg). Click Calculate Diameter to perform the calculation.

    Example 2: Calculate the diameter of the top string on a ukulele in inches.

    I want my uke's top string to have a tension of 8 lbs. Click on the first string and enter the number 8 in the text field (you can leave out the lbs). Click Calculate Diameter to perform the calculation.
