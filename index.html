    Instructions

    Home

    Step by step instructions

    • Choose an instrumental group (bowed or plucked)
    • Select your instrument from the list on the following page
    • Change the relevant options for your instrument - material, pitch and sting length
    • When you're happy with this, click ok to go to the calculations page
    • The calculations page lists the stings on the selected instrument in descending order (highest to lowest)

    Calculating the tension

    Example 1: Calculate the tension of the top string on a lute in kg.

    The highest pitched sting on a lute usually has a diameter of around .44 mm. Click on the first string and type 44 in the text field that appears (leave out the decimal point and unit of measurement). Click Calculate Tension to perform the calculation.

    Example 2: Calculate the tension of the top string on a guitar in lbs.

    Let's say the highest string on my guitar is 0.028 inches in diameter. Click on the first string and just enter 0.028 in the text field. Click Calculate Tension to get the tension.

    Calculating the diameter

    Example 1: Calculate the diameter of the e string on a baroque violin in mm.

    Let's say I want to find out how thick a string should use at a a tension of 6 kg. Click on the first string and enter the number 6 in the text field (you can leave out the kg). Click Calculate Diameter to perform the calculation.

    Example 2: Calculate the diameter of the top string on a ukulele in inches.

    I want my uke's top string to have a tension of 8 lbs. Click on the first string and enter the number 8 in the text field (you can leave out the lbs). Click Calculate Diameter to perform the calculation.

    How the calculator works

    This app will help you to calculate the tension and/or the diameter of the strings on your instrument.

    In order to perform these calculations we need to first know the density of the material, the length of the string and the frequency of the pitch.

    After selecting your instrument you'll be able to review these options required to perform your own calculations.

    Different materials

    The provided densities for the various string materials are as accurate as the information I could find!

    Nylon may vary slightly in density between manufacturers and gut strings may feel like they have a higher or lesser tension depending on the thickness of the string and how the string has been prepared.

    It's difficult to calculate the density of a wound string, as it depends on both the material in the core and the outer winding. This varies from maker to maker. Most commercial retailers seem reluctant to make this information available. No wound string densities are provided for this reason.

    String lengths

    The string lengths provided are averages only. As string lengths vary between instruments you'll get the most accurate results if you measure the string length of your own instrument.

    N.B. the string length is measured as the distance between the nut and the bridge of the instrument.

    Choice of pitch

    Historical musicians often tune to different pitches according to what music they're performing. Modern instruments are tuned to 440 Hz, or thereabouts.

    If you're not sure about this setting, leave it as is!
